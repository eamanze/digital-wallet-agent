## 1. Why Are We Doing This? (The Vision)

**The core vision is simple:**  
To move our youth from **learning a skill** to **earning an income** – and to create a **self-sustaining cycle** where successful youth give back to fund the next generation.

We are not just organizing a training. We are building a **pipeline** that looks like this:

> **Training (3 Saturdays) → Shortlisting → Business Pitch → Grant Award → Income → 10% Returns to Fellowship**

This ensures:
- Youth gain practical skills
- The most serious and creative ones receive startup support
- The fellowship eventually becomes **self-funding** through the 10% income-sharing model

| Problem | Our Response |

| Even with a skill, they lack capital to start | Business grants for top participants |
| Without accountability, grants are wasted | Pitch competition with clear criteria |

## 5. What Happens After the 3 Saturdays? (The Pitch & Grant)

This is the most important part – where we separate serious participants from casual attendees.

### Step 1: Shortlisting

From each skill track (e.g., tie & dye, soap making, pastry, etc.), we select **2 participants** based on:

- Attendance (must attend at least 2 of 3 Saturdays)
- Practical competence (their final project or product)
- Attitude and commitment during training

**Total shortlisted:** 2 persons × number of skill tracks (e.g., 5 tracks = 10 people; 10 tracks = 20 people)

---

### Step 2: Business Pitch Event

On a separate day (could be during a youth conference or a special Saturday), shortlisted participants present their business ideas to a panel of judges.

**What they pitch:**
- What product or service they will offer
- Who their customers are
- How much money they need (small grant – e.g., ₦50,000 – ₦100,000)
- How they will use the money
- How they will repay 10% of their monthly income for 6 months

---

### Step 3: Judges & Criteria

**Panel of judges (3–5 people):**
- Youth Pastor (PYP)
- One business owner from the church
- One representative from a foundation (if available)
- One financial literacy person

**Judging criteria (100 points total):**

| Criteria | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Clarity of business idea | 20 |
| Feasibility (can it really work?) | 20 |
| Passion and presentation | 15 |
| How they will use the grant | 15 |
| Understanding of customers | 15 |
| Willingness to repay 10% | 15 |
| **Total** | **100** |

---

### Step 4: Grant Award

- **Number of winners:** Top 3–5 participants (depending on available funds)
- **Grant amount per winner:** ₦50,000 – ₦100,000 (to be decided)
- **Disbursement:** Given after signing a simple agreement

## 6. The 10% Income回流 Model (Sustainability)

This is what makes the program **self-sustaining**.

| Agreement | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Each grant winner agrees | To return **10% of their monthly net income** from the business |
| Duration | For **6 months** after receiving the grant |
| Minimum monthly return | No fixed minimum – they report honestly |
| Maximum monthly return | No cap – they can give more if business does well |
| Where the money goes | Back into the fellowship to fund the next cohort’s grants |

**Example:**  
A winner receives ₦100,000 grant. Their business makes ₦50,000 profit in month one. They return ₦5,000 (10%). Over 6 months, they might return ₦30,000 – ₦50,000 total. That money funds the next participant.

**Benefits:**
- Teaches financial discipline and gratitude
- Creates a revolving fund – the program becomes cheaper each time
- Winners become partners, not just beneficiaries
